What ingredient makes sour candy sour?

citric acid One of the most common ingredients in sour candy is citric acid. As you can probably guess from the name, this sour acid occurs naturally in citrus fruits like lemons and grapefruits, and can also be found in smaller amounts in berries and some vegetables.

Why do Warheads taste like vomit?

The secret to the Warhead’s sourness lies in its combination of organic acids. The outer layer contains malic acid, an excruciatingly tart compound that can be found in smaller doses in Granny Smith apples. Inner layers contain tamer citric acid and ascorbic acid (better known as vitamin C).
