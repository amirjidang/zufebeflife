The Rock's Daughter Has No Idea Her Dad Is Moana's Maui As They Sing Along Together

 Posted on August 22, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  387 words  |  Patria Henriques

The Rock's daughter demands her dad sing along to Moana's "You're Welcome" blissfully unaware that her dad is also Maui.

We can only begin to imagine what it's like to have a famous parent. On the one hand, there are huge benefits. Depending on the fame and success of that parent, their children may never want for anything if they don't have to. They can also get a leg up into the same industry if needed. On the not-so-bright side, they will be thrust into the world of showbiz whether they like it or not.

What we had never really considered is a child not even realizing their mom or dad is famous. For the first few years of their life, that will be the case, no matter how famous their parents might be. That was demonstrated perfectly by The Rock who posted a video of him singing along to Moana's "You're Welcome" with his daughter, Jasmine.

For those who haven't seen Disney's Moana (seriously, where have you been?) The Rock plays demi-God Maui. Maui sings "You're Welcome" and you would think that's why Rock's four-year-old daughter insists he sings the song with her every time it's on. However, in the caption beneath the clip, the former WWE Superstar reveals Jasmine has no idea he is the voice behind the song.

"And for the 937th time today she wants daddy to sing along with Maui.

She has no idea, we’re the same person," Rock wrote on Instagram. He also admitted to having no idea what day it is and shouting out all the other parents who are unexpectedly at home right now looking after their kids 24/7 as this global pandemic continues on.

Rock is one of the busiest men in show business, so must feel more cooped up than most right now as he is not able to leave the house. Having your daughter insist on watching a movie you starred in over and over again must be quite the experience too. Probably pretty cool and extremely flattering the first ten times, but not so much as you round the corner of the 1000th viewing.
