Atlanta-based rapper Trinindad James has been styling and profiling in the rap game for years and that's because he thinks of his body as an art canvas -- always ready to be filled with color.

Trinidad James has done amazing works in his career. Even after being dropped from Def Jam Recordings, he kept on working and making music. Later, he was Signed by Artist Publishing Group, for his songwriting skills and his rapping. Trinidad James has a net worth of $5 million, which he has earned from all his works.

James is a very famous personality of Trinidad, who has mostly worked in America. He is also a citizen of America, because of his work and time spent. James was involved in music from a very young age, and soon after shifting to Florida, he started making more and more songs. He signed a Major deal with Def Jam Recordings, in the year 2012.

At that time, it became quite sensational news, and his popularity also rose. In 2014, he was removed from the recordings, as he failed to deliver any album. Till date, James has many mixtapes, singles, music videos, and collaborations, but he hasn’t released any albums.

According to TMZ, “We caught the "All Gold Everything" rapper Wednesday outside LAX, and we couldn't help but notice his latest manicure ... and had to get a gauge on where he was taking this trend.”

Plenty of rappers paint their nails these days, but Trinidad says he's the top innovator and all his nail techs across the globe know it.

He says one perk of being a pioneer is never having to pay top price, but admits he sometimes spends upwards of $2,500 to make his fingertips bling.

On the music scene, Trinidad tells us he just laid down a joint with T-Pain and is putting the finishing touches on "Don't Be S.A.F.E. 2" -- the sequel to his debut project from 2012.

We also spoke about his friendship with Rich Homie Quan which dates back 10 years, both being native ATLiens who blew up the local rap scene during their respective come-ups.

RHQ recently expressed to TMZ Hip Hop how the "shoot 'em up/bang-bang" music needs to be put on a cease-fire. Well, Trinidad says he can easily fix that issue ... and he has the all-gold solution right around his neck.
