'Mission Impossible': The Cast Ranked From Richest To Poorest

Since its inception in 1996, the Mission Impossible franchise has captivated audiences around the world with its heart-pounding action sequences, intricate plots, and a revolving door of talented actors who have brought the series' iconic characters to life.

Led by Tom Cruise's charismatic performance as Ethan Hunt, the franchise has become synonymous with thrilling espionage adventures and jaw-dropping stunts. Let's take a closer look at the main actors who have contributed to the franchise's success, the characters they portrayed, and how they rank by net worth, of course!

11 Tom Cruise As Ethan Hunt In Mission: Impossible ($600 Million Net Worth)

At the epicenter of the Mission: Impossible franchise stands Tom Cruise, the consummate action star, whose portrayal of Ethan Hunt has defined the series. Hunt is the quintessential secret agent, known for his resourcefulness, unwavering dedication to his missions, and propensity for performing death-defying stunts. Cruise's real-life commitment to performing his own stunts has become a hallmark of the franchise, adding an unparalleled level of authenticity to the action sequences.

Tom Cruise has a net worth of $600 million, making him one of the highest-paid actors in the world. This immense wealth is a testament to his success not only in the Mission: Impossible franchise but also in his extensive filmography spanning decades. Cruise's dedication to his craft and his ability to consistently deliver thrilling performances have affirmed his status as a Hollywood icon. Moreover, his involvement in the production and development of the Mission: Impossible films has allowed him to shape the franchise according to his vision, resulting in a series that continues to captivate audiences worldwide.

10 Jeremy Renner As William Brandt In 2011 And 2015's Mission: Impossible ($80 MIllion Net Worth)

Jeremy Renner joins the franchise as William Brandt, an IMF analyst with a mysterious past. Renner's character brings a unique dynamic to the team, combining his intelligence and combat skills to complement Ethan Hunt's daring escapades. Brandt's evolution over his appearances adds depth to the ensemble and showcases the franchise's ability to introduce new compelling characters. Renner nearly took over the franchise in 2011, when Tom Cruise was tentatively scheduled to leave the Mission: Impossible films.

Jeremy Renner has a net worth of $80 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. Renner's success in the film industry extends beyond his role in the Mission: Impossible series. He has also gained recognition for his performances in other notable films, such as The Hurt Locker (2008), for which he received an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor, and The Avengers. Both critics and fans alike have highly praised Renner's performance as Hawkeye in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

9 Alec Baldwin As Alan Hunley In 2015 And 2018's Mission: Impossible ($70 Million Net Worth)

Alec Baldwin takes on the role of Alan Hunley, the director of the CIA and a recurring character in the franchise's recent installments. Baldwin's character provides a bureaucratic counterpoint to the IMF's unconventional methods, adding an extra layer of tension and complexity to the team's missions.

Alec Baldwin has a net worth of $70 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. His portrayal of Alan Hunley showcases his skill as an actor, seamlessly transitioning from his comedic roles to a more serious and authoritative character. Baldwin's commanding presence and sharp wit bring depth and credibility to the character, making him a formidable opponent for Ethan Hunt and his team.

With his sharp suits and stern demeanor, Baldwin embodies the CIA's no-nonsense approach to intelligence gathering and national security. His dynamic interactions with the rest of the cast create compelling on-screen chemistry and further elevate the tension and suspense of the mission.

8 Jon Voight As Jim Phelps In 1996's Mission: Impossible ($55 Million Net Worth)

In the inaugural film of the franchise, Jon Voight took on the role of Jim Phelps, the leader of the Impossible Mission Force (IMF) team. As the mentor to Ethan Hunt, Voight's character plays a pivotal role in setting the stage for the franchise's espionage-laden narrative. His complex and enigmatic character sets the tone for the series' blend of trust, betrayal, and moral ambiguity.

Jon Voight has a net worth of approximately $55 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. Born on December 29, 1938, in Yonkers, New York, Voight began his acting career in the late 1960s and quickly rose to prominence with his powerful performances. He has been recognized with numerous accolades, including an Academy Award for Best Actor for his role in the 1978 film Coming Home. Voight's versatility as an actor is evident in his ability to seamlessly transition between genres, from intense dramas to action-packed thrillers.

7 Henry Cavill As August Walker In 2018's Mission: Impossible ($50 Million Net Worth)

Henry Cavill makes a memorable appearance in Mission: Impossible: Fallout as August Walker, a CIA assassin. Cavill's physicality and imposing presence create a formidable adversary for Ethan Hunt. The intense choreography of the bathroom fight scene, featuring Cavill's Walker, stands as a testament to the franchise's commitment to pushing the boundaries of action.

Henry Cavill has a net worth of approximately $50 million, thanks to his successful acting career and endorsement deals. He first gained recognition for his role as Superman in the DC Extended Universe films, including Man of Steel and Batman v. Superman: Dawn of Justice. Cavill's portrayal of the iconic superhero earned him a loyal fanbase and solidified his status as a Hollywood leading man.

6 Philip Seymour Hoffman As Owen Davian In 2006's Mission: Impossible ($25 Million Net Worth)

In Mission: Impossible III Philip Seymour Hoffman delivered a memorable performance as Owen Davian, a ruthlessly cunning arms dealer. Hoffman's portrayal added a layer of intensity and danger to the franchise, making him one of the most memorable antagonists Ethan Hunt faces. His calculated demeanor and psychological manipulation test Hunt's resolve and resourcefulness.

Phillip Seymour Hoffman had a net worth of $25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. Despite his untimely death in 2014, Hoffman's impact on the film industry continues to be felt through his incredible body of work. From his Oscar-winning performance in Capote to his scene-stealing roles in films like The Big Lebowski and Boogie Nights, Hoffman's talent made him an adored and respected actor.

5 Ving Rhames As Luther Stickell In 1996 And 2022's Mission: Impossible ($25 Million Net Worth)

Ving Rhames is a constant presence throughout the franchise, reprising his role as Luther Stickell in multiple films. As a tech expert and loyal friend to Ethan Hunt, Rhames' character provides essential support to the team. His humor and camaraderie with Hunt offer a sense of continuity and familiarity as the franchise evolves.

Ving Rhames has a net worth of $25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. Whether it's cracking codes or providing crucial information, Stickell's contributions have been instrumental in the success of the Impossible Missions Force (IMF).

4 Simon Pegg As Benji Dunn In 2006 And 2023's Mission: Impossible ($25 Million Net Worth)

Simon Pegg's Benji Dunn adds a dose of humor and relatability to the intense world of Mission: Impossible. Initially introduced as a lab technician, Benji's character evolves into a field agent, showcasing Pegg's versatility as both a comedic and action-oriented actor. His interactions with Ethan Hunt provide levity amidst the high-stakes missions.

Simon Pegg has a net worth of $25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. He has achieved success not only through his role in the Mission: Impossible franchise but also through his work in other popular films such as Hot Fuzz and Shaun of the Dead.

3 Michelle Monaghan As Julia Meade-Hunt In 2006, 2011, And 2018's Mission: Impossible ($13 Million Net Worth)

Michelle Monaghan's Julia Meade-Hunt plays a significant role as Ethan Hunt's wife. Her presence humanizes Hunt, providing a glimpse into his personal life and vulnerabilities. The emotional depth Monaghan brings to the character adds a layer of resonance to the action-packed narratives, reminding audiences of the stakes beyond the espionage.

Michelle Monaghan has a net worth of $13 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. Her portrayal of Julia Meade-Hunt has garnered critical acclaim, with her performance praised for its complexity and emotional range. Monaghan's chemistry with Tom Cruise's Ethan Hunt is palpable on screen, creating a believable and compelling relationship. As the series progresses, Julia's character evolves, facing her own challenges and becoming a formidable force in her own right.

2 Rebecca Ferguson As Ilsa Faust In 2015 And 2018's Mission: Impossible ($6 Million Net Worth)

Making her debut in Mission: Impossible: Rogue Nation, Rebecca Ferguson's Ilsa Faust is a skilled and enigmatic British agent. Her complex allegiances and remarkable combat abilities make her a standout character in the series. Ferguson's portrayal brings a refreshing sense of agency and intrigue, showcasing the franchise's commitment to strong and multifaceted female characters.

Rebecca Ferguson has a net worth of $6 million. Her success in the Mission: Impossible series has undoubtedly contributed to her financial success. 1 Vanessa Kirby As Alanna Mitsopolis / White Widow In 2018's Mission: Impossible ($4 Million Net Worth)

Vanessa Kirby's Alanna Mitsopolis, also known as the White Widow, is a key figure in Mission: Impossible: Fallout, her enigmatic character straddles the line between ally and manipulator. Vanessa Kirby has a net worth of $4 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.
