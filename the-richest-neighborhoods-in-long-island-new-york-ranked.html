Long Island, located in New York State, is home to some of the richest neighborhoods in the United States. From luxurious estates to sprawling mansions, the island boasts some of the most opulent and sought-after real estate in the country. Let’s explore the nine richest neighborhoods on Long Island and discover what makes them so desirable. From waterfront properties with private beaches to charming villages with top-rated schools and exclusive country clubs, Long Island's affluent enclaves offer a lifestyle of luxury and prestige. Let’s take a closer look at the wealth, glamor, and beauty of these luxurious communities.

Amagansett - $2,200,000

Although Amagansett has many attractions, its beaches stand out as the most popular feature. In fact, Indian Wells Beach, Main Beach, and Atlantic Avenue Beach were named the best beaches in the United States by Dr. Beach in 2013. In addition to its beaches, Amagansett boasts the Amagansett National Wildlife Refuge, which spans 36 acres and serves as a natural sanctuary for a variety of rare animals and plants. Once a hub for fishing and whale-watching, the hamlet now features several commercial fishing operations and world-class seafood restaurants.

Oyster Bay Cove - $2,300,000

During the 17th century, English and Dutch colonists settled in Oyster Bay Cove, unaware that it would eventually transform into a prosperous neighborhood with serene and picturesque natural surroundings. One of the many advantages of living in Oyster Bay Cove is its close proximity to Muttontown Preserve and Caumsett State Historic Park Preserve. These locations offer hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and breathtaking views for both residents and visitors to enjoy.

Muttontown - $2,400,000

Nestled in Nassau County, Muttontown is a highly sought-after village renowned for its picturesque charm. The neighborhood boasts tranquil landscapes, a storied past, and luxurious estates. Historically, Muttontown was a hub of the thriving mill industry in New York. Nowadays, the area is known for its expansive properties, some of which are home to horses and farm animals. For golf enthusiasts, Muttontown is a dream come true. The Muttontown Club boasts a championship 18-hole golf course that offers stunning views of the area.

Sands Point - $2,900,000

During the early 1900s, affluent American families began constructing opulent vacation homes in Sands Point, further enhancing the area's allure and leading to the development of an affluent community. As word spread about the scenic beauty of Sands Point, its value continued to soar. Sands Point, situated on a peninsula in Nassau County, boasts stunning waterfront views that enchant its residents. The area is home to a plethora of recreational and educational facilities, including the esteemed Sands Point Golf Club.

Mill Neck - $3,100,000

Mill Neck is a coveted neighborhood on Long Island, renowned for its exclusivity and unique combination of natural splendor, quaint ambiance, and cultural affluence. The area is home to several historic landmarks, including the Oyster Bay Railroad Museum. This museum highlights the essential role that the railroad played in shaping the lives of Mill Neck's residents. In addition to the Oyster Bay Railroad Museum, Mill Neck is also known for its beautiful nature preserves and parks. The Mill Neck Creek runs through the area, providing stunning views and recreational opportunities for residents and visitors alike.

Water Mill - $4,000,000

Water Mill, located in the eastern region of Long Island, is a quaint village renowned for its splendid natural surroundings, including picturesque farmland, stunning beaches, and historic dwellings. It's a perfect getaway for those seeking refuge from the fast-paced urban lifestyle. In addition to its scenic beauty, Water Mill boasts high-end resorts and top-notch restaurants, making it a haven for both locals and tourists. Furthermore, Water Mill is home to some of the most opulent and expensive homes in the United States.

Quogue - $4,100,000

Quogue's affluence is further amplified by its tight-knit and exclusive community. One can't help but admire Quogue for its magnificent attractions, particularly its stunning beaches. Additionally, the neighborhood is strategically located between Quantuck Bay and the Atlantic Ocean, offering its inhabitants breathtaking vistas of these renowned water bodies. With a median home price of $4,100,000, Quogue is one of the most expensive places to live in the country. However, its residents believe that the lifestyle and amenities that the neighborhood offers are well worth the cost.

Sagaponack - $4,300,000

Sagaponack, situated in the southern region of Long Island, stands out as one of the wealthiest neighborhoods in the area, and it undoubtedly clinches the top spot on our list. Its vast expanse comprises breathtaking beaches and expansive farmlands. Upon visiting Sagaponack, one can't help but marvel at the opulent oceanfront mansions that adorn the region, some of which rank among the most expensive residences, not just in New York but across the entire nation. In addition to its natural splendor, Sagaponack boasts the Sagaponack Historic District, which has earned a spot in the National Register of Historic Places.

North Haven - $5,800,000

North Haven, the wealthiest neighborhood on Long Island, is renowned for its breathtaking vistas, picturesque surroundings, and welcoming community, drawing in some of the most affluent New York residents. With a population of less than a thousand as of 2020, North Haven fosters a close-knit and engaging atmosphere. Some of the properties in North Haven boast waterfront locations, featuring spacious houses with meticulously tended lawns. These luxurious residences flaunt a fusion of classic and contemporary architectural styles.
