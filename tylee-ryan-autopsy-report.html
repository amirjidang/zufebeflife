Tylee Ryan Autopsy Report: Forensic Pathologist Testifies In Trial

 Posted on August 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  531 words  |  Martina Birk

The trial of Lori Vallow Daybell has been ongoing for weeks now, with witnesses and evidence being presented to shed light on the deaths of JJ Vallow and Tylee Ryan. 

In the latest development in the case, forensic pathologist Dr. Garth Warren testified about Tylee Ryan autopsy report, providing new details and insights into her tragic death.

Tylee Ryan was just 16 years old when she went missing in September 2019.

Her remains were eventually discovered on Chad Daybell’s property in June 2020, along with the remains of her younger brother JJ Vallow. 

The discovery of their bodies sparked a nationwide search for the truth behind their deaths and led to the arrest of Lori Vallow Daybell and Chad Daybell.

During his testimony, Dr. Warren revealed that the cause of Tylee Ryan’s death was determined to be a homicide, though the exact means by which she was killed remain unspecified. 

Warren described her remains as severely charred and destroyed, with only a collection of bones with mud and dirt remaining.

Examining the remains

During the autopsy, Warren and his team looked for evidence of a weapon or other debris that could provide clues to Tylee’s cause of death but found none. 

They performed a variety of tests but could find no evidence to support the theory that Tylee was alive when her body was burned.

Although the exact cause of Tylee’s death remains a mystery, Warren testified that there were no signs of disease or trauma to her internal organs, including her ribs and lungs. 

He also mentioned that her body was wrapped in bags when it was discovered, though he did not provide further details on this aspect of the investigation.

What the autopsy tells us

The autopsy report provides new insights into the circumstances surrounding Tylee Ryan’s death, though much remains unknown. 

Nevertheless, the report reveals that Tylee’s remains suffered severe damage, suggesting that whoever killed her went to great lengths to conceal their crime.

The report also underscores the importance of forensic science in uncovering the truth behind crimes such as this. 

Without a thorough examination of Tylee’s remains, it is unlikely that the authorities would have been able to determine the cause of her death, let alone bring her killer (or killers) to justice.

Moving forward

The trial of Lori Vallow Daybell continues, with the prosecution presenting its case against the accused. 

Evidence presented thus far paints a disturbing picture of a mother and stepfather who were willing to go to extreme lengths to cover up their crimes. 

However, many questions remain unanswered, and it is unclear whether the case will ultimately be resolved to the satisfaction of all involved.

In the meantime, the memory of Tylee Ryan and JJ Vallow lives on, reminding us of the importance of justice and the value of human life. 

While the circumstances of their deaths may never be fully understood, their story serves as a powerful reminder of the need for vigilance and accountability in the face of such senseless tragedy. 

As this case unfolds, we can only hope that justice will be served and that the memories of these two young people will be honored in some way.
