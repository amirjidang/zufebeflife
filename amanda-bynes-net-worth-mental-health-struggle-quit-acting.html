Why Did Amanda Bynes Quit Acting?

Amanda Bynes stopped having fun acting and that was the catalyst to quit

Amanda Bynes leaving Hollywood was something that left many people sad, as her work was often highly favored. In addressing this step back, the former actress tweeted about her retirement in 2010.

"I don't love acting anymore so I've stopped doing it," Bynes wrote.

Growing up in the entertainment industry can be quite detrimental to one's mental health, and unfortunately, Bynes was not immune to this side effect.

Not long after stepping away from the spotlight, Amanda unfortunately had a public breakdown while in her mid-20s. In response to this, she was put in a psychiatric hold and her mom ended up taking conservatorship over her. This conservatorship lasted almost a decade, from 2013 through 2022. All throughout that time, Bynes went through a rollercoaster of emotions in her ongoing battle with mental health.

Towards the end of her time in the spotlight, Amanda Bynes turned to substances, which seemed to be the beginning of the end of her career.

"I started smoking marijuana when I was 16. Even though everyone thought I was the 'good girl,' I did smoke marijuana from that point on," Bynes said. "I didn't get addicted (then) and I wasn't abusing it. And I wasn't going out and partying or making a fool of myself … yet."

How Amanda Bynes Earns Money Now

Amanda Bynes works as a manicurist after getting her degree in cosmetology

Amanda Bynes' conservatorship ended in March 2022. With her newfound freedom, the former star decided to find a new purpose for her life. She decided to go to school to get a license in cosmetology, specifically to become a nail artist.

Bynes posted on her Instagram story in October 2022, "In cosmetology college to become a manicurist!" She also posted a video to show her followers a glimpse of the facility she was attending so they could see what she was learning while in school.

She also shared that she was passionate about continuing her education.

"I am continuing with my Bachelor's Degree at FIDM, majoring in Creative Industry Studies with a core in Beauty Marketing and Product Development," Bynes explained. "I am traveling to New York in June to work on developing a fragrance which will be ready to launch near the holidays."

Besides these ventures, Bynes has also been turning back to music. She has been earning some income through the music industry after releasing two singles in the last few years.

In 2021, she partnered with Precise to release the song "Diamonds." In 2022 she and her then-fiance Paul Michael made the song "Fairfax."

Bynes recently started a podcast with Paul Sieminski. How that will fare and whether it will be an income producer remains to be seen.
