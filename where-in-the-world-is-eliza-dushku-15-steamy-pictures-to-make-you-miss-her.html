On Buffy The Vampire Slayer

Okay, but let’s talk about Eliza Dushku’s character on Buffy the Vampire Slayer because she was MEGA hot.

Eliza Dushku’s Faith was introduced during season three of Buffy the Vampire Slayer. In the fantasy world of BTVS, there was only one vampire slayer at a time. When one slayer died, a new slayer would get her powers. Makes sense, but things got screwy. In season one, Buffy drowned for a hot minute. At that point, Kendra got her powers. Because of this, there were two active slayers in season two, even though it was only supposed to be one slayer at a time. Near the end of season two, Kendra was killed by Darla, who truly may have been one of the most annoying vampires of all time. Kendra’s death then activated Faith’s slayer powers, which is how both Buffy and Faith came to be slayers at the same time.

As a character, Faith was a perfect foil to Buffy. Being that Buffy came from a relatively happy home, it was easy for her to take on the role of the good guy, making the right decisions and not taking advantage of her powers. Faith, however, came from a troubled background, which made her resolve to the good side very shaky.

Eliza Dushku's Faith was intended for a five-episode arc, but her character was so popular that she stayed on throughout season three. Faith also returned for a two-episode arc in season four and the final stretch of the series in season seven.

Eliza Dushku In Dollhouse

After Bring It On, Eliza Dushku appeared in Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back, The New Guy, and Wrong Turn, none of which were strong enough to blast her into the next stage of her film career. However, her television career was still going strong. Dushku continued appearing on Buffy the Vampire Slayer. She also appeared in six episodes of the BTVS spin-off, Angel. From there, Eliza Dushku’s next biggest television role was in Joss Whedon’s Dollhouse. Dushku played Echo, a “doll” who would be programmed with a personality, complete a task for a client, then have her memory and personality wiped clean. The show followed Echo on her journey towards self-awareness. So, Westworld before Westworld. Though, the show didn’t receive the same critical acclaim as Westworld and was cancelled after only two seasons.

Still, the idea of Eliza Dushku dressing up as a different character with a different personality in every episode should have been enough for, like, six seasons, no?
