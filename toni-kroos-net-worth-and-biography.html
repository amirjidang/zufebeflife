Toni Kroos Biography

Toni kroos was born on the 4th of January, 1990 to former badminton and football trainer Birgit Kammer (mother) and Roland Kroos (father) in Greifswald, Germany. The German midfield Maestro had a comfortable life while growing up, he was loved by everyone around him, as he was well behaved and humble.

Toni kroos started his senior career by playing for Bayern Munich at age seven, in 2007. He later sort for game time else where as he moved to Bayer Leverkusen on loan since he was rarely used at his parent club. At Leverkusen, Toni Kroos put up excellent performances for the German side which increased his profile, hence on return to his parent club Bayern Munich in 2010, he became a regular for the club helping the club to claim the UEFA Champions League title and two DFB-Pokal titles.

In 2014, Toni Kroos signed for Spanish side Real Madrid for €25 million. He became very pivotal to the club under the leadership of coach Carlo Ancelotti, still after Ancelotti was succeeded by Coach Zinedine Zidan he still became indispensable to the coach and the team as a whole. Toni Kroos helped Real Madrid to claim the UEFA Champions league title three consecutive times, and also the LA Liga title in 2016-2017 campaign. He was also named the German Footballer of the Year in 2018 due to his outstanding performances for club and country.

Internationally, Toni kroos won the Golden player award and emerged the highest goal scorer in the UEFA European U-17 Championship in 2006. In the 2007 FIFA U-17 World cup, Toni Kroos won the Golden Ball award for best player. Three years after claiming the Golden Ball award, Toni Kroos was promoted to the Germany senior team, scoring his first goal for Germany in a friendly match against Poland in 2011. In 2014, the prolific midfielder helped his Country to win the world cup title, in which he was the assist leader of the tournament.
Toni Kroos married his girlfriend, Jessica Farber, in 2015. They have two children, Leon and Amelie.
