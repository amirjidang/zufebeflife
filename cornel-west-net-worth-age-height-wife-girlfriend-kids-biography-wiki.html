Early Life & Biography

He was born Cornel Ronald West on 2nd June in the year 1953. He took birth in a place called Tulsa in Oklahoma in the United States. His father’s name was Clifton Louis West Jr., and he used to work with the Department of Defense as a general contractor. His mother was an elementary school teacher and later on went to become the principal. Her name was Irene.

In the year 1970, he completed his schooling and went to the prestigious Harvard University for further studies. From there, he graduated magna cum laude in Near Eastern languages and civilization.

After this, he got into another prestigious institution called Princeton University to complete his Ph.D. degree. In the year 1980, he earned a Ph.D. degree in philosophy, and by this, he created history by becoming the first African-American to get a Ph.D. degree from the institution.

Career

In the year 1994, Harvard University decided to appoint West as a professor of African-American studies at the University. Along with this, he was also appointed at the Harvard Divinity School, and in the year 1998, he became the first Alphonse Fletcher University Professor at Harvard.

In the year 2002, he returned to Princeton. In Princeton, he helps to build a world-class center for African-American studies. In the year 2012, he again joined the Union Theological Seminary but is still associated with Princeton as Professor Emeritus. In the year 2016, Harvard announced that he was going to return to the University.

Apart from the scholarly work, he has also worked in movies and shows. One of his major acting work was when he played the role of Councillor West in the famous Matrix movie franchise. He has published many of his literary works, including Race Matters, published in 1993 and deals with different social science topics.

Awards & Achievements

Cornel West, in all of his work, has achieved success. He has earned many achievements and received many awards. He won the Lannan Cultural Freedom Prize Award in the year 2005. He was bestowed upon the Fields Memorial Award for Social Justice by Princeton University in 2010.

He has also received American Book Award. These were some of the awards which he has received in his illustrious career. Apart from this, he has also been given multiple honorary degrees.
