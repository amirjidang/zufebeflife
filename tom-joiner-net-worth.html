Radio Broadcasting in the 1970s and 80s

After graduating from college, Tom Joyner embarked on his professional radio career in the 1970s. He honed his skills at various radio stations across the South and Midwest regions before making a significant move to Chicago in 1978. In the Windy City, Joyner hosted the morning show at WVON, leaving a lasting impression with his charismatic and engaging on-air personality.

Over the years, Joyner continued to make waves in the radio industry, showcasing his talents on stations such as WBMX-FM and WJPC-AM. However, it was in 1983 that he returned to his roots in Dallas to host the morning show on KKDA-FM. This marked the beginning of an era as Joyner not only dominated the Dallas airwaves but also became known as the “Fly Jock” due to his commuting between Dallas and Chicago for eight years.

Tom Joyner’s remarkable career in radio broadcasting throughout the 1970s and 80s laid the foundation for his future success. His dedication to delivering quality content and connecting with his audience set him apart, solidifying his position as one of the industry’s most influential figures.

Personal Life and Honors

Tom Joyner, apart from his successful career in radio and philanthropic endeavors, has also had a rich personal life. He was married to Dora Chatmon and has two sons named Thomas Jr. and Oscar.
